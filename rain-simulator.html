RAIN SIMULATOR

2026

Rain Simulator is an interactive installation that uses a rain stick as a physical controller to change the intensity of digital rain. Built in TouchDesigner, the project abstracts rainfall into a visual and responsive system, transforming a simple weather simulation into a ritual-like experience of gesture, sound, and atmosphere.

TouchDesigner / Interactive Installation

Rain stick sounds were classified in Teachable Machine based on tilt angle and movement speed. In TouchDesigner, I used changes in sound frequency and rhythm to control the visual intensity of the rain.

Additional sound-visualization studies exploring alternative ways to translate rain stick sounds into abstract motion, texture, and atmosphere.
