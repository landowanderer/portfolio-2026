ARTIFICIAL ATMOSPHERE

2026

Artificial Atmosphere is a moving image and immersive experience design project that explores how generative AI translates weather into cinematic atmosphere rather than physical reality. Through a series of atmospheric short films, VR experiences, prints, and installation formats, the project examines rain, fog, and spatial distortion as visual systems for AI-driven worldbuilding.

ARTIFICIAL ATMOSPHERE: LA RAIN transforms real footage through generative AI, using rain as both a meteorological and emotional layer. The film observes how algorithmic exaggeration of light, haze, and texture dissolves everyday spaces into unstable cinematic environments.

These visual studies document the generative process behind LA RAIN, analyzing how source footage mutates across different AI models, text prompts, and simulated atmospheric conditions.

ARTIFICIAL ATMOSPHERE: FOG continues this weather-based worldbuilding, utilizing generative AI’s structural misreadings of space and logic as part of the film’s visual language. Through fog, these algorithmic distortions become a medium to explore spatial uncertainty and dreamlike perception.

The DVD objects re-materialize LA RAIN and FOG as physical artifacts, extending the AI-generated videos beyond the screen. Through format, surface, and display, these prints explore how physical design can amplify the atmosphere of algorithmic image-making.

The installation translates the project into a spatial experience, contrasting the immersive depth of VR exploration with the nostalgic, physical presence of CRT monitors.
